## Industry Standard Features

### Core capabilities across SAP Ariba, Coupa, Tipalti, Serrala:
1. **PO visibility** — vendor sees all POs issued to them; can convert a PO directly into an invoice
2. **Invoice submission** — vendor submits invoice against a PO with line-item detail
3. **Status tracking** — real-time visibility of invoice status (submitted, under review, approved, paid)
4. **Document history** — full audit trail of all transactions
5. **Notifications** — email alerts when invoice status changes or is returned

### UX Best Practices:
- PO-to-invoice conversion flow: vendor finds the PO, clicks "Create Invoice," and the form pre-fills from the PO — reduces data entry errors
- Status is always visible and self-explanatory — reduces vendor inquiry calls to AP team by 60–75%
- Returned invoices show clear rejection comments so vendor knows exactly what to fix

### Authentication:
- Invite-only is the industry standard — buyer sends invite to vendor email; vendor registers via the invite link
- Tied to vendor's master record — one portal account per vendor company

## Thailand-Specific Notes
- Thai tax invoices must include vendor's 13-digit taxpayer ID and VAT registration number
- WHT certificate (ใบรับรองหักภาษี ณ ที่จ่าย) is expected by vendors after payment — consider adding this to Phase 2
- e-Tax Invoice submission via Revenue Department's system is becoming mandatory for larger companies — future consideration

## Impact Data (Ardent Partners, State of AP 2025)
- Top-performing teams hit 52.8% touchless invoice processing (up from 47.2% in 2024)
- A vendor portal is a key driver — reduces vendor inquiry calls by 60–75%
- Mid-sized company (2,000 invoices/month): portal reduces AP inquiry calls from 150–200 to 30–50 per month
